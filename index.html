Quick Guide
Getting Started
Scan an IP or Domain
Type any IPv4, IPv6, or domain name into the search bar and click Scan ↗ or press Enter. Results load in a few seconds.
Check Your Own IP
Click My IP to auto-fill and scan your current public IP address.
Share a Result
After a scan, a Share bar appears with a direct link. Hit Copy to grab it — anyone opening the link will land straight on that result.
Result Sections
Identification & Geo
Country, city, timezone, coordinates, hostname, and whether the address is IPv4 or IPv6. Dual-stack lookups show both addresses.
Network (ASN)
The ISP or hosting provider, Autonomous System Number, and the registered CIDR range the IP belongs to. Hosting/datacenter IPs will show the cloud provider here.
Privacy & Threat Flags
Quick-read badges for VPN, proxy, Tor exit node, relay, datacenter, mobile, and crawler detection. Each flag is sourced from multiple providers and weighted into the overall risk score.
WHOIS / RDAP
Registration details for the IP block — registrar, org name, country, and abuse contact email pulled from regional internet registries.
Shodan
Open ports and running services detected by Shodan's internet-wide scans. Shows service banners and version strings where available — useful for spotting exposed infrastructure.
Blacklist / DNSBL
Checks the IP against dozens of DNS-based blocklists used by mail servers and firewalls. A hit here means the IP has a spam or abuse history.
VirusTotal
Aggregated malware and threat engine verdicts from VirusTotal's 90+ security vendors. Shows the detection ratio and individual engine results.
Domain Intelligence
Appears only when scanning a domain. Shows domain WHOIS registration data and VirusTotal URL/domain scan results alongside the IP data.
Risk Score
The score (0–100) is calculated from threat flags, DNSBL hits, VirusTotal detections, and Shodan exposure. Higher is riskier. Color coding: green is clean, yellow is suspicious, red is a known threat. The score also appears in the scan history for quick reference.
AI Analysis
Gemini AI Threat Summary
After a scan loads, click Analyse with Gemini AI (or the shortcut button at the top of results). Gemini receives all the collected data and returns a plain-English threat assessment with context — useful for understanding what the numbers actually mean.
Tip: AI analysis is a separate on-demand step and does not run automatically, so you only use the quota when you need it.
Top Bar
History
Stores your last 50 scans in this browser. Click any entry to instantly reload that result. Use the ✕ next to an entry to remove it, or Clear all to wipe the full list.
Settings
All scanning features are pre-configured and work out of the box with no setup needed. In Settings you can optionally enter your own API keys for VirusTotal, Shodan, ipinfo.io, and Proxycheck to use your personal quotas instead of the shared worker keys. Keys are stored in this browser only.
